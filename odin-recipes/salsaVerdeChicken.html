Salsa Verde Chicken

Description

This recipe uses chicken thighs or breasts cooked in homemade salsa verde with cumin, onion powder, chopped jalapeno, chopped garlic, scallions, and cilantro to make a flavorful and spicy dish that goes well over rice or on tortillas.

This recipe can also be served with avocado and sour cream to add a bit of creaminess and balance the heat, if you are not used to spicy foods.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs of chicken breast or thighs
  • Kosher salt and black pepper
  • 1 3/4 cups salsa verde
  • 4 cloves of garlic, chopped
  • 1 jalapeno, chopped
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 2 teaspoons oregano
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 4 scallions, sliced
  • 1 small bunch of cilantro, chopped finely
  • 1 tablespoon of honey

Steps

  1. Arrange chicken breasts on a cutting board, cover with plastic wrap, and using a meat tenderizer or rolling pin, pound the breasts into an even ½-inch thickness. Season chicken with ¼ teaspoon salt and ½ teaspoon pepper; add to slow-cooker with salsa verde, garlic, jalapeno, cumin, and onion powder.
  2. Stir to combine, cover and cook on high for 4 hours or on low for 6-8 hours.
  3. Using two forks, shred chicken into bite-sized pieces. With heat on low, add scallions, cilantro, and honey; stir to combine. Season to taste with salt. Serve with rice or in tortillas with desired toppings.
